SECTION 1 — WHAT DATA WE COLLECT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Nothing.

We collect nothing. Not a byte. Not a nibble. Not even the ghost of a bit
lingering wistfully in a buffer somewhere. Our servers do not receive your
name, your age, your location, your device model, your operating system
version, your hopes, your fears, your lunch order, or your opinion of us.

We do not collect:

  - Personal identifiers (name, email, address, phone, fingerprint, aura)
  - Device information (model, OS, battery level, screen brightness)
  - Usage data (which buttons you tapped, how long you stared at a screen)
  - Location data (GPS, IP-based, triangulated, vibes-based, or spiritual)
  - Behavioural data (clicks, scrolls, hesitations, regrets)
  - Biometric data (face, voice, gait, the particular way you sigh)
  - Financial information (we do not want your money, only your flourishing)
  - Health information (please see a doctor; they will traffick you far more
    thoroughly than we ever could)
  - Communications (we have not read your messages; we cannot read messages)
  - Preferences and interests (we do not know what you like, nor do we ask)
  - Children's data (see Section 5 for our equally emphatic nothing on this)

In the interest of full transparency: there is a strong possibility that
during your use of our applications, absolutely nothing was transmitted
to us. We have reviewed our servers and can confirm they remain blissfully
unaware of your existence. They are doing fine. They do not miss you.

SECTION 2 — HOW WE USE YOUR DATA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We do not use your data.

This section exists because privacy policies are legally expected to contain
a section explaining how data is used. We have included this section out of
deep respect for the genre. We hope you appreciate the effort it took to
write a section about using something we do not have.

Should you be concerned that we are using your data in ways you have not
authorised, we invite you to consult Section 1, which we believe addresses
the matter comprehensively.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3 — HOW WE STORE YOUR DATA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We do not store your data.

Any data generated by your use of our applications — such as your personal
settings, saved state, or preferences — exists exclusively on your own
device, in your own storage, under your own control, for your own purposes,
in your own life, which we genuinely hope is going well.

This data does not travel anywhere. It sits quietly on your device like a
well-trained houseplant, growing only in the direction of your own
convenience, drawing sustenance from nothing we have provided.

We do not have a database. We do not have a server receiving user records.
We do not have a spreadsheet with your name on it. We do not have a
Post-it note with your details scrawled on it. We do not have a filing
cabinet. We are not secretly old-fashioned.

Our applications do not collect data from anyone, including children.

  - The right to access your data: granted (there is nothing to see)
  - The right to correct your data: granted (nothing is already accurate)
  - The right to delete your data: granted (done; it was never here)
  - The right to data portability: granted (nothing is very portable)

SECTION 7 — DATA SECURITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We protect your data using a method that security researchers have long
considered theoretically ideal but practically impossible to implement
at scale: we do not have it.

It is genuinely difficult to breach, leak, misplace, sell, expose in a
regrettable press release, or accidentally attach to a reply-all email
a dataset that does not exist. We have stress-tested this approach
extensively. Results are consistently excellent.

In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected users promptly.
As there is no data, there can be no breach. As there can be no breach,
there will be no notification. This section therefore closes without
incident. We thank you for your attention to it.

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy in the future.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
APPENDIX B — A NOTE ON THE LENGTH OF THIS DOCUMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

You may have noticed that this privacy policy is quite long for a document
that is ultimately communicating one fact: we collect nothing.

This is because the genre of the privacy policy demands length, structure,
numbered sections, and formal register, regardless of the quantity of data
practices being described. A privacy policy that simply said "we collect
nothing, goodbye" would be technically accurate but would not be taken
seriously by review boards, app stores, or compliance frameworks.

We have therefore written a full, formal, extensive, and thoroughly
considered Privacy Policy about nothing.

We hope you found it informative.
